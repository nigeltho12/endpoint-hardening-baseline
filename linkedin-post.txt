This weekend I built something I've been wanting to document properly for a while.

A dual-platform endpoint hardening baseline — Windows and macOS side by side — with CIS Benchmark controls, documented rationale for each one, and PowerShell / Bash audit scripts you can actually run.

Here's why I built it the way I did:

Most hardening checklists tell you *what* to configure. They don't explain *why the control exists* or *what the threat is* if you skip it. That gap matters, especially in environments where you're explaining decisions to leadership or justifying policy to engineers who are going to push back.

So every control in this repo has three things:
— What the setting does technically
— What attack or risk it mitigates
— How to audit the current state without touching anything

The audit scripts output PASS / FAIL / MANUAL to give you a clear picture of where an endpoint stands before you make a single change.

I also put together a comparison document mapping equivalent controls across both platforms — particularly useful for orgs running Intune + Jamf in parallel. The gaps section at the end covers what I see most often: FileVault not enforced while BitLocker is, macOS endpoints missing from the SIEM pipeline, SIP disabled on dev machines with no compensating controls.

Tech stack: PowerShell, Bash, CIS Benchmark v3.0 (Windows 11 + macOS 14 Sonoma)

Repo is live: github.com/nigeltho12/endpoint-hardening-baseline

If you're managing a mixed-OS fleet or building out an endpoint security program, feel free to fork it and adapt it to your environment.
